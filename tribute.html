Betty Holberton

  • 1917- Born in Philadelphia, Philadelphia County, Pennsylvania, USA.
  • During 1940s Studied journalism.
  • 1946- One of the six original programmers of the first general-purpose electronic digital computer, ENIAC.
  • 1950- Wrote the first statistical analysis package used for the 1950 US census.
  • 1953-She was made a supervisor of advanced programming in a part of the Navyâ€™s Applied Math lab in Maryland.
  • 1997-Received the Augusta Ada Lovelace Award, the highest award given by the Association of Women in Computing.
  • 1997-Received the IEEE Computer Pioneer Award from the IEEE Computer Society for developing the sort-merge generator
  • 1997-She was inducted into the Women in Technology International Hall of Fame, along with the other original ENIAC programmers.
  • 2015-The Holberton School, a project-based school for software engineers based in San Francisco, was founded in her honor.
  • 2010-She died in Rockville, Maryland.

During her time working on ENIAC she had many productive ideas that came to her overnight, leading other programmers to jokingly state that she "solved more problems in her sleep than other people did awake."
